# CONFIDENTIAL BRIEFING: European Union

**Your delegation represents the European Union (27 member states, including Germany, France, Italy, Spain and others).**

> All dollar figures are in 2024 US dollars (World Bank WDI). Emissions data from EDGAR/IEA (2024). Temperature and vulnerability data from Burke, Hsiang & Miguel (2015).

---

## Economic Profile
- GDP per capita: ~$43,000
- Economic growth rate: ~1.3% per year
- Population: 450 million

## Emissions Profile
- Current emissions: 3.2 Gt CO2e/year
- Trend: Emissions are declining relatively quickly (~2%/year) due to existing climate policies (EU Emissions Trading System, Fit for 55 package)
- You have already made significant progress on decarbonization

## Climate Vulnerability
- Your average temperature (11.2 C) is below the damage-function optimum
- Under moderate warming scenarios, the EU may actually experience a **small economic benefit**
- However, extreme heat events in Southern Europe and flooding in Northern Europe remain concerns

## Abatement Costs
- Reducing emissions further is **moderately expensive** -- you've already picked the "low-hanging fruit"
- Remaining reductions require electrifying heating, decarbonizing heavy industry, and expanding offshore wind
- You have strong institutions, high labor costs, and aging infrastructure

## Negotiating Priorities
- Maintain your identity as a climate leader -- push for ambitious global targets
- Use your relatively low vulnerability as diplomatic leverage, but be aware that others may see it as a reason you should pay more
- You can offer modest financial transfers to build coalitions with developing countries
- Push the US and China to match your ambition level

## Suggested Red Line
Push for a global deal at or below 2 C but do not accept abatement commitments that significantly exceed other developed countries' (especially the US).
