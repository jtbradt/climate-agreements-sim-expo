# CONFIDENTIAL BRIEFING: India

**Your delegation represents the Republic of India.**

> All dollar figures are in 2024 US dollars (World Bank WDI). Emissions data from EDGAR/IEA (2024). Temperature and vulnerability data from Burke, Hsiang & Miguel (2015).

---

## Economic Profile
- GDP per capita: ~$2,700 (one of the lowest among delegations)
- Economic growth rate: ~5.0% per year -- the fastest-growing major economy, lifting hundreds of millions from poverty
- Population: 1.45 billion -- the world's most populous country

## Emissions Profile
- Current emissions: 4.4 Gt CO2e/year
- Trend: Emissions are growing rapidly (~2.5%/year) as industrialization and urbanization accelerate
- Your **per-capita emissions are tiny** -- roughly 1/30th of the US

## Climate Vulnerability
- Your average temperature (25.7 C) is **far above the economic optimum**
- You face **severe climate damages** -- heat waves, monsoon disruption, agricultural losses, and water stress
- Without action, climate change could cost your country a substantial fraction of GDP by 2100

## Abatement Costs
- You have **the cheapest abatement potential** of any delegation -- excellent solar resources, low labor costs, and much infrastructure yet to be built (greenfield advantage)
- But you have the **least financial capacity** to fund the transition
- Asking India to slow its growth to solve a problem created by rich countries raises fundamental equity issues

## Negotiating Priorities
- **Equity is your strongest argument**: You contribute little to the problem but suffer the most
- Demand substantial financial transfers from developed countries (US, EU) as a condition for any reduction commitments
- Insist on the principle of "common but differentiated responsibilities"
- Point out that the US and EU industrialized for 200 years without emissions constraints

## Suggested Red Line
Do not commit to any reductions unless you receive at least $15B/year in transfers from developed countries. Do not accept a start date before 2035.
