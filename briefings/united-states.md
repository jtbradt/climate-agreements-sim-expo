# CONFIDENTIAL BRIEFING: United States

**Your delegation represents the United States of America.**

> All dollar figures are in 2024 US dollars (World Bank WDI). Emissions data from EDGAR/IEA (2024). Temperature and vulnerability data from Burke, Hsiang & Miguel (2015).

---

## Economic Profile
- GDP per capita: ~$85,000 (highest among all delegations)
- Economic growth rate: ~1.5% per year
- Population: 340 million

## Emissions Profile
- Current emissions: 5.9 Gt CO2e/year -- the second-largest emitter
- Trend: Emissions are declining slowly (~0.5%/year) due to market forces (coal-to-gas switching, renewable deployment)
- Your per-capita emissions are among the highest in the world

## Climate Vulnerability
- Your country's average temperature (13.6 C) is near the economic optimum in climate models
- You face relatively **low climate damages** from moderate warming
- Extreme weather events are a concern but GDP growth effects are small

## Abatement Costs
- Reducing emissions is **moderately expensive** -- you have extensive natural gas infrastructure, sprawling transportation systems, and a large petrochemical industry
- You have excellent renewable energy potential (Great Plains wind, Southwest solar) and deep capital markets

## Negotiating Priorities
- Protect economic competitiveness -- avoid committing to costly reductions unless major emitters (especially China) make comparable commitments
- Resist large transfer payments to developing countries
- You can argue for "market-based solutions" and technology partnerships rather than direct payments

## Suggested Red Line
Do not commit to transfers exceeding $20B/year without comparable commitments from the EU and China on emissions.
